60-Minute Meeting

Structure and suggested content

0–10 MIN

Welcome & Connection

Greeting: "Thank you for being here. Let's start with: How was your child when they left for school?"

Invite 2-3 parents to share
Bridge: "Today we discuss bullying prevention as a community"
10–25 MIN

Understanding Bullying

Key point: Bullying = Repetition + Power Imbalance

Three roles: Victim, Aggressor, Bystander (the majority)
Many children witness bullying without knowing what to do
Distribute Warning Signs Guide
25–40 MIN

Parent Reflection

Activity: Read one Empathy Card. Ask: "What would YOUR child do?"

Small group discussion (5 min)
Share with full group (5 min)
40–55 MIN

What Parents Can Do

Message: "Create space for conversation, not expertise."

Activity 1 • 15 min • Any day

Best Part & Hardest Part

At dinner: Everyone shares one good thing and one difficult thing. No judgment, no advice.

Activity 2 • 10 min • Weekend

The Forbidden Question

Ask: "Is there anyone no one talks to? Why?" Shows their social awareness.

Activity 3 • 20 min • Monthly

Relationship Check-In

Ask them to name 3 people who make them smile at school. If they can't, contact school.

Have parents sign Family Commitment Letter TODAY
55–60 MIN

Closing

"No school solves bullying alone. You are our most important partners."

Share communication channel for reports
Thank all parents, especially first-timers

⚠️ Warning Signs

Behavioral changes to watch for

Child may be a VICTIM

Avoids school

Stomach aches, vague excuses

Tense with messages

Uncomfortable receiving texts

Missing items/money

Comes home hungry, no explanation

Withdrawn

Cries easily, lost interest

No friends mentioned

No one to sit with at lunch

Sleep problems

Nightmares, wakes exhausted

Child may be an AGGRESSOR

Talks negatively

Laughs about humiliating others

Must always "win"

Frustrated losing even at home

New money/objects

Can't explain where from

Lacks empathy

Indifferent to others' suffering

If you see signs: Create a calm moment: "I've noticed you haven't been doing well. I'm here." Then contact school.
