60-Minute Meeting Outline

Structure, content, and suggested lines for facilitators

0–10 MIN • OPENING

Welcome & Connection

Greeting: "Stand to greet parents. Thank you for being here. Let's start with a simple question:"

"How was your child when they left for school today?"
Invite 2-3 parents to share briefly

Bridge: "Today we're discussing how ALL of us can prevent bullying—not as someone else's problem, but as something we address together."

10–25 MIN • INFORMATION

Understanding Bullying

Key Definition: Bullying = Repetition + Power Imbalance

One conflict or mean comment isn't bullying, but a pattern is
The three roles: Victim, Aggressor, Bystander (the majority)

Critical insight: "Your child might be WITNESSING bullying without knowing what to do. That's the real gap we're addressing today."

Distribute Warning Signs Guide now
25–40 MIN • REFLECTION

Dynamics with Parents

Activity: Choose one card from the Empathy Cards deck

Read it aloud. Ask parents: "What would YOUR child do if they saw someone humiliated?"
Small group discussion (3-4 people, 5 minutes) — NO right answers
Open to whole group for 5 minutes. Listen for patterns.
40–55 MIN • ACTION

What Parents Can Do

Message: "You don't need to become an expert. You need to CREATE SPACE for conversation."

Three at-home activities parents can start THIS WEEK:

Activity 1 • 15 minutes • Any day

The Best Part & Hardest Part Game

At dinner: Everyone shares one good thing that happened today and one difficult thing. No judgment, no advice—just listening.

Why it works: Creates a habit of daily conversation. Child learns they CAN tell you hard things.

Activity 2 • 10 minutes • Weekend

The Forbidden Question

Ask: "Is there anyone in your class that almost NO ONE talks to? Why do you think that happens?"

Why it works: Shows you their social landscape. Shows their level of empathy.

Activity 3 • 20 minutes • Once monthly

The Relationship Check-In

Ask them to name 3 people who make them smile at school. Talk about why they matter.

Why it works: If they CAN'T name anyone, that's an important signal to contact us immediately.

Distribute the Family Commitment Letter—have parents sign it TODAY

⚠️ Warning Signs Guide

Behavioral changes parents should watch for and address

Signs your child may be a VICTIM

😰 Avoids going to school

Frequent stomach aches, headaches, or vague excuses

📱 Tensión with messages

Gets uncomfortable when receiving texts, looks away from phone

🍽 Missing items or money

Comes home hungry or without lunch money, no clear explanation

😔 Withdrawn behavior

Cries easily, lost interest in activities they loved

👥 Stopped mentioning friends

Says no one to sit with at lunch, hasn't mentioned classmates

😴 Sleep problems

Nightmares, wakes exhausted despite sleeping many hours

Signs your child may be an AGGRESSOR

😤 Talks about classmates negatively

Laughs about getting someone in trouble or humiliating them

💪 Must always "win"

Gets extremely frustrated losing even at home games

💸 New money/objects

Can't explain where they came from

🙈 Lacks empathy

Shows indifference when others are hurt or upset

If you see signs: DON'T accuse. Instead, create a calm moment: "I've noticed you haven't been doing well. You don't have to tell me everything now, but know I'm here." THEN contact the school.
