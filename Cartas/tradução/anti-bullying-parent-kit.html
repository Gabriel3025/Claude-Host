Parent Meeting Outline (60 minutes)

0–10 MINUTES • OPENING

Welcome and Icebreaker

Stand to greet parents and start with: "How was your child when they left for school today?"

Let 2-3 parents respond to warm up the room
Present agenda: "Today we discuss bullying prevention together"
10–25 MINUTES • INFORMATION

What is and isn't bullying

Key point: Bullying = repetition + power imbalance

Show 3 profiles: victim, aggressor, bystander
"Your child might be witnessing bullying without knowing what to do"
Distribute Warning Signs Guide now
25–40 MINUTES • REFLECTION

Dynamics with parents

Activity: Choose a reflection card and ask: "What would your child do if they saw someone humiliated?"

Small group discussion (3-4 parents, 5 minutes)
Open to full group - no right answers, just reflection
40–55 MINUTES • ACTION

What parents can do at home

Message: "You don't need to be experts - just create space for conversation"

ACTIVITY 1: Best Part/Hardest Part game at dinner
ACTIVITY 2: Ask about excluded classmates
ACTIVITY 3: Monthly relationship check-in
Distribute Family Commitment Letter for signatures
55–60 MINUTES • CLOSING

Thank You

"No school solves bullying alone. You are our most important partners."

Share your communication channel for confidential reports
Thank all parents, especially first-time attendees

⚠️ Warning Signs Guide

For parents: Bullying is rarely reported directly. Watch for behavioral changes.

Signs your child may be a victim:

😰 Doesn't want to go to school

Frequent stomach aches or headaches

📱 Gets tense receiving messages

Uncomfortable with phone around you

🍽 Missing items or money

Comes home hungry with no clear explanation

😔 Became withdrawn

Cries frequently, lost interest in activities

👥 Stopped mentioning friends

No one to sit with at lunch

😴 Sleep difficulties

Nightmares or wakes exhausted

Signs your child may be an aggressor:

😤 Talks deprecatingly about classmates

Laughs when someone gets in trouble

💪 Needs to "win" in everything

Extremely frustrated when losing

💸 New money or objects

Can't explain where they came from

🙈 Can't put themselves in others' shoes

Reacts with indifference to others' suffering

If you identify signs: Don't confront with accusations. Create a calm moment: "I've noticed you haven't been doing well. I'm here." Then contact the school.

3 At-Home Activities

⏱️ ACTIVITY 1: Best Part & Hardest Part (15 min, any day)

At dinner: everyone shares one good thing and one difficult thing. No judgment, just listening.

Why: Creates habit of daily conversation. Child learns they can tell you difficult things.

⏱️ ACTIVITY 2: The Forbidden Question (10 min, weekend)

Ask: "Is there anyone in your class that almost no one talks to? Why do you think that happens?"

Why: Helps you understand your child's social landscape and their empathy level.

⏱️ ACTIVITY 3: Relationship Check-in (20 min, monthly)

Ask them to name 3 people they feel good with at school. Talk about why they're important.

Why: If they can't name anyone, that's an important signal to contact the school.

Phrase to print for refrigerator:
"Children who feel heard at home are more likely to ask for help when they need it."
