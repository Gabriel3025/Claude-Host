In Another's Shoes

01
TIME
20 min
AGE
4th–9th
GROUP
Whole class
OBJECTIVE
Develop the ability to see situations from another person's perspective.
HOW TO DO IT
1
Ask each student to remove one shoe and place it in the center of the circle.
2
Each student picks up another classmate's shoe and holds or tries it on.
3
Ask: "What would it be like to walk one day in this shoe?"
4
Each student briefly shares what they imagined.
FINAL REFLECTION
"What did this teach you about judging others before knowing them?"

02
TIME
25 min

Individual
OBJECTIVE
Strengthen self-esteem and self-compassion after difficult situations.
HOW TO DO IT

The Web of Empathy

03
TIME
15 min
AGE
4th–9th
GROUP
Whole class
OBJECTIVE
Show how actions affect everyone — and how empathy connects people.
HOW TO DO IT
1
Seat the class in a circle. One student holds a ball of yarn.
2
They say something positive and toss the yarn, holding the thread.
3
Continue until everyone is connected by the web.
4
Ask one to let go — show how the entire web is affected.
FINAL REFLECTION
"What happens when someone is excluded or treated badly?"

Traffic Light of Emotions

04
TIME
20 min
AGE
4th–7th
GROUP
Whole class
OBJECTIVE
Teach emotional self-regulation using the traffic light symbol.
HOW TO DO IT
1
Red = stop and breathe. Yellow = think. Green = act calmly.
2
Read conflict situations and ask which light they're at.
3
In pairs, practice what to do at each light.
4
Share strategies with the class.
FINAL REFLECTION
"Which light do you spend most time at? What helps you move forward?"

Mirror of Emotions

05
TIME
15 min
AGE
4th–6th
GROUP
Pairs
OBJECTIVE
Recognize emotional expressions and respond with empathy to feelings.
HOW TO DO IT
1
Divide students into pairs facing each other.
2
One shows an emotion through facial expression and body language.
3
The other mirrors the expression and names the emotion.
4
Switch roles and repeat with different emotions.
FINAL REFLECTION
"How did it feel to mirror someone's emotion? Did you understand them better?"

The Listening Circle

07
TIME
25 min
AGE
5th–9th
GROUP
Whole class
OBJECTIVE
Develop active listening and respectful communication skills.
HOW TO DO IT
1
Sit in a circle. One student speaks about a personal experience for 2 minutes.
2
Others listen without interrupting or planning their response.
3
After, listeners repeat what they heard and ask clarifying questions.
4
Rotate until 3-4 students have shared.
FINAL REFLECTION
"How did it feel to be truly heard? What made someone a good listener?"
